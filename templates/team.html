Who are we? 🤓

We are Elissa Day and Asker Kurt-Elli, two graduate students at Harvard University from the UK and Ireland.


Elissa is a PhD student in Harvard University’s Near Eastern Languages and Cultures (Egyptology) programme. 📜 She obtained her BA in Egyptology from the University of Liverpool, where she also worked for the Careers and Employability department. She then studied at Selwyn College, Cambridge, and Indiana University, where she taught Egyptian hieroglyphs. Elissa is passionate about diversity and inclusion, in particular, access to postgraduate education. 🏫 In her spare time, she likes to play baseball, write, and be continually disappointed in her fantasy football team. 🏈

Asker is the Michael von Clemm Fellow at Harvard University and a research assistant at the Berkman Klein Center for Internet and Society. 💻 He did his BA in Music at Magdalen College, Oxford where he received the Gibbs Prize, before completing an MPhil in Digital Humanities at Trinity Hall, Cambridge. 🎶 As someone going through the PhD application process currently, he has a vested interest in this project! Outside of academia, he is a keen musician, climber, surfer, and cook of vegan takes on traditional Cypriot and Mauritian dishes. 🍲
